WHAT ARE CYBERATTACKS?

Cyberattacks are when hackers or malicious users try to steal, damage, or access information without permission. These attacks can target anyone who uses technology, especially teenagers.

They often happen through phones, computers, or social media and may involve spreading harmful software, stealing data, or using the internet to harm others.

For teens, common online threats include phishing, cyberbullying, and online predatory behavior.

Phishing uses fake messages or posts to trick people into giving away passwords or personal details.

Cyberbullying involves using digital platforms to spread rumors, share private content, or send hurtful messages.

Online predatory behavior happens when adults pretend to be someone else to form fake relationships with teens.

These online threats can seriously affect a teenager’s privacy, reputation, and emotional health, showing why digital awareness is so important today.

WHY ARE TEENAGERS VULNERABLE TO CYBERATTACKS?

Teenagers are more vulnerable to cyberattacks because their brains and behaviors are still developing. Child psychologist Jeremiah Dickerson explains that a teenager’s brain is different from an adult’s because it is “reward and attention seeking,” which makes teens “impulsive by nature.”

The part of the brain that encourages risk-taking develops early, while the part that controls decision-making doesn’t fully mature until around age 24.

This means teenagers are more likely to take risks online or act without thinking about the long-term consequences.

Many teens also feel pressure to fit in with their peers, which can lead them to overshare personal information or participate in risky online trends.

As technology continues to grow, teenagers are leaving behind more digital footprints, making it easier for hackers to find and misuse their information.

All these factors make teens easier targets for cyberattacks and online manipulation.
